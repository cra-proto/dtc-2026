Medical practitioners who can certify impairments

Applying for the DTC involves the person who has the impairment and a medical practitioner who can certify the effects of the impairment.

There are many types of medical practitioners who can complete and certify the effects of the impairment.

Medical practitioners who can certify impairments
Medical doctor All impairments
Nurse practitioner All impairments
Optometrist Vision
Audiologist Hearing
Occupational therapist Walking, feeding, dressing
Physiotherapist Walking
Psychologist Mental functions
Speech-language pathologist Speaking

Completing the DTC application

Applicants complete Part A of the DTC application, and medical practitioners complete Part B. Applicants may apply for the DTC using the digital form or the printed paper form.

Part B must only be completed by the medical practitioner. Applicants cannot complete Part B themselves.

How to assess patients

The CRA bases its decision about eligibility on the information that medical practitioners provide in Part B of the DTC application.

Use your best professional judgment when filling out Part B.

You may base your assessment on:

  • the symptoms reported by the patient
  • your knowledge of the patient's medical history
  • your direct observation of the patient’s limitations
  • your knowledge of the effects of the impairment(s) as they relate to the patient's medical condition

How to complete Part B

If the applicant has completed part A online or by phone, they will get a reference number you can use to complete Part B online.

If the applicant has completed Part A on paper, you must complete Part B on the same paper form.

If the applicant has not completed Part A yet, you can complete part B on paper or online and print out the form for them to complete part A.

Completing Part B online
  1. Open the digital DTC application for medical practitioners
  2. If you have a reference number

    The person with the impairment or their legal representative may fill out Part A of the digital application form. At the end of Part A, the applicant will receive a reference number. The number is valid until the medical practitioner submits Part B. The reference number, last name, and date of birth must match what was submitted in Part A.

    1. Enter the reference number provided by your patient.
    2. Answer a few questions about your patient and the impairment(s):
      • Basic information about you
      • Patient name and date of birth
      • Information about the effects of the impairment(s)
    3. After Part B is completed, the digital form is automatically submitted to the CRA. You have the option to save a copy of Part B for your records
  3. If you do not have a reference number
    1. Fill out part B of the form using the Digital application for medical practitioners
    2. Print out the form and sign it
    3. Give the form to your patient
    4. Your patient must then fill out Part A and submit the completed form T2201 to the CRA.
  4. The CRA may contact your office to verify the source of the application.
    5. Read the privacy statement

The information collected may be disclosed to other federal, provincial, territorial, aboriginal, or foreign government institutions to the extent authorized by law. Completing Part B on the paper form
  1. Answer a few questions and completes only the impairment section(s) that apply to your patient:
    • Basic information about you
    • Patient name and date of birth
    • Information about the effects of the impairment(s)
  2. After completing Part B, sign the "Certification" section on the last page.

Submit the application

There are 2 ways to submit a completed Form T2201, Disability Tax Credit Certificate, and any related documents to the CRA.

Online

If you have a PDF or scanned version of the form, the person with the impairment may sign into My Account and submit the form by selecting Submit documents.

By mail

If you have the paper form, you or the applicant must send it to one of the tax centres below. It is recommended that you keep a copy for your records.
