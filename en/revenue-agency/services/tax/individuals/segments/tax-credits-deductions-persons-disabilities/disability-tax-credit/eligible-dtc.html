Who is eligible

You may be eligible for the DTC if a medical practitioner certifies any of the following:

  • You experience a severe and prolonged impairment (a marked restriction) in 1 category
    What marked restriction means
    • You are unable to do the activity, or it takes 3 times longer than someone of similar age without the impairment, even with the use of appropriate therapy, medication, and devices
    • This restriction is present all or almost all of the time (generally at least 90%)
    • The restriction has lasted or is expected to last for a continuous period of at least 12 months
  • You experience significant limitations in 2 or more categories
    What significant limitation means
    • The 2 or more limitations exist together all or almost all of the time (generally at least 90%)
    • If the limitations were combined, their cumulative effect (combined impact) would be both of the following:
      • equivalent to being unable, or taking 3 times longer than someone of similar age without the impairment, to do an activity in 1 of the categories
      • present all or substantially all of the time (generally interpreted as 90% or more), even with the use of appropriate therapy, devices, and medication
  • You receive therapy to support a vital function

Getting the DTC is not based on:

  • a diagnosis you received
  • whether or not you can work
  • your age

We determine your eligibility only based on the restrictions in physical or mental functions you experience that the medical practitioner certifies.

Categories of impairment

Choose a category to find out if your situation may qualify for the DTC as a marked restriction in one category or a life-sustaining therapy.

Combining 2 or more categories

The effects of 2 or more impairments combined may make you eligible, when 1 impairment on its own does not meet the criteria for a marked restriction and is not life-sustaining therapy.

Learn more: Cumulative effect of significant limitations

Eligibility for other provincial or federal disability payments

Your eligibility for the DTC does not depend on other federal or provincial benefits you receive. If you receive Canada pension plan or Quebec pension plan disability benefits, workers’ compensation benefits, or other types of disability or insurance benefits, it does not necessarily mean that you are eligible for the DTC, because different programs have different criteria.

However, if you are eligible for the DTC, you may be eligible for certain other payments.
