How the review process works

We review applications based on the information provided by the medical practitioner.

  1. [We receive your application]
  2. [We do the initial review, comparing the details in your application with the DTC eligibility criteria]
  3. [We may ask for additional information]

    In some cases, we may send the medical practitioner a letter asking for more information, such as documents to support your application. The letter will contain a reference number and instructions on what to do.

  4. [If we asked for additional information, we review that information]
  5. When our review is complete, we send you a letter (notice of determination) that tells you CRA's decision.

If you send your application with your tax return, we will review your application before assessing your income tax return.

How long it takes

We try to process your application and send you a notice of determination as quickly as possible. It may take longer if information is missing.
